|**Background:**|•<br>**Kevzara, Tocilizumab**<br>`o`<br>Kevzara and Tocilizumab are recombinant humanized interleukin-6 (**IL-**<br>**6**) receptor inhibitors that works to inhibit IL-6 mediated actions at<br>soluble and membrane bound IL-6 receptors. Inhibiting the signaling<br>pathway can lead to inhibition of activated T-and B-cells, lymphocytes,|

||monocytes, and fibroblasts. IL-6 is also produced by synovial and<br>endothelial cells which has an effect on the inflammatory process.<br>•<br>**Bimzelx, Cosentyx, Siliq, Taltz**<br>`o`<br>Bimzelx, Cosentyx, Siliq, and Taltz are human IgG1 monoclonal<br>antibodies. Cosentyx and Taltz selectively bind to the interleukin-17A<br>(**IL-17A**) cytokine and inhibits its interaction with the IL-17 receptor. Siliq<br>binds with the**IL-17 receptor**. Bimzelx binds to**IL-17A, IL-17F, and IL-**<br>**17AF**. IL-17 is a naturally occurring cytokine that is involved in normal<br>inflammatory and immune responses.<br>•<br>**Adalimumab, Enbrel, Infliximab, Cimzia, Simponi**<br>`o`<br>Enbrel, adalimumab, infliximab, Cimzia, and Simponi are tumor necrosis<br>factor (**TNF**) inhibitors, which results in an interference in the production<br>of downstream inflammatory mediators, including interleukin-1,<br>prostaglandins, platelet activating factor, and nitric oxide. TNF, a<br>naturally occurring cytokine, mediates inflammation and modulates<br>cellular immune responses.<br>•<br>**Otezla**<br>`o`<br>Otezla is an oral phosphodiesterase 4 (**PDE-4**) inhibitor specific for cyclic<br>adenosine monophosphate (cAMP). PDE4 regulates immune and<br>inflammatory processes through control of intracellular cAMP levels and<br>downstream protein kinase A pathways. The production of a number of<br>key inflammatory cytokines is affected by PDE4 including interferon<br>(IFN)γ, tumor necrosis factor (TNF)α, interleukin (IL)-12, and IL-23, thus<br>shaping the immune response. PDE4 inhibition results in increased<br>intracellular cAMP levels and an inhibitory effect on multiple cytokines<br>involved in the inflammatory process.<br>•<br>**Stelara**<br>`o`<br>Stelara is a human immunoglobulin G (IgG) monoclonal antibody that<br>binds with high affinity and specificity to the p40 subunit, which is part of<br>both interleukin (IL)-12 and IL-23.**IL-12**and**IL-23**are naturally occurring<br>cytokines that are involved in inflammatory and immune responses, such<br>as natural killer cell activation and CD4+ T-cell differentiation and<br>activation. Stelara binding to the p40 subunit prevents IL-12 and IL-23<br>from binding to the IL-12 receptor which is the ligand binding subunit of<br>the receptor complexes. Prevention of IL-12 and IL-23 from binding to<br>their respective complexes disrupts IL-12 and IL-23 transduction.<br>•<br>**Litfulo, Olumiant, Xeljanz, Rinvoq**<br>`o`<br>Litfulo, Olumiant, Xeljanz, and Rinvoq are orally bioavailable, small-<br>molecule inhibitors of the Janus kinase (**JAK**) family. These medications<br>modulate the signaling pathway at the point of JAKs, preventing the<br>phosphorylation and activation of Signal Transducers and Activators of<br>Transcription (STATs), which modulate intracellular activity including<br>gene expression.<br>•<br>**Kineret**<br>`o`<br>Kineret blocks interleukin 1 (**IL-1**) alpha and beta by competitively<br>inhibiting IL-1 binding to the interleukin-1 type I receptor (IL-1RI), which<br>is expressed in a wide variety of tissues and organs. IL-1 production is<br>induced in response to inflammatory stimuli and mediates various<br>physiologic responses including inflammatory and immunological<br>responses.<br>•<br>**Orencia**<br>`o`<br>Orencia is a fully human recombinant fusion protein categorized as a co-<br>stimulatory or second-signal blocker of**T cell**activation. Orencia disrupts<br>the activation pathway of T cells causing a disturbance in key<br>mechanisms of inflammation and progressive joint destruction.|

||• <br>**Ilumya, Omvoh, Tremfya, Skyrizi** <br>`o` <br>Ilumya, Omvoh, Tremfya, and Skyrizi are human monoclonal antibodies<br>that selectively antagonize interleukin 23 (**IL-23**) to inhibit the release of<br>pro-inflammatory cytokines and chemokines.<br>•<br>**Sotyktu**<br>`o`<br>Sotyktu is an inhibitor of tyrosine kinase 2**(TYK2)**, a member of the<br>Janus kinase (JAK) family, and results in inhibition of downstream<br>activation of Signal Transducers and Activators of Transcription<br>(STATs). This results in inhibition of interleukin (IL)-12, IL-23 and type 1<br>interferon (IFN) pathways which are implicated in multiple immune-<br>mediated diseases.<br>•<br>**Velsipity, Zeposia**<br>`o`<br>Zeposia is a sphingosine 1-phosphate**(S1P)**receptor modulator that<br>binds with high affinity to S1P receptors 1 and 5, and Velsipity is a S1P<br>receptor that binds with high affinity to S1P receptors 1, 4, and 5.
